Key Terms Deciphered! Action Steps for You!
Activist Investor An activist investor is an investor who purchases large numbers of a public company’s shares (usually in an attempt to get a seat on its board) to try to effect changes in the company that they believe will be positive for the share price. This can be good or bad for the company, often depending on whether the activist’s plans offer potential for long-term value or simply a short-term boost to the share price. The stock price will likely increase in the short run!
Approval Approval from a Regulatory Body to go through with a purchase of another company, to get a patent or license to sell a drug Getting an approval likely means the stock price will skyrocket! Consider buying the stock
Bear Market A bear market is a general term used to describe a market in which a type of investment is going down in value over a period of time
Benchmark Refers to a basket of stocks that refelct the average of all companies in it. For e.g An index like the S&P500.
Blue Chip Stock A blue-chip stock is that of a large, established, stable company that’s been around for a while and is therefore generally able to keep trucking along in good times and bad.
