Banking and Finance Services

Overview - Industry offers services like lending, insurance and securities management

Products include credit cards, mortgages, loans, insurance, and checking/savings accounts

Customers can be segmented by income levels, individual vs small businesses vs large businesses

Highly competitive market: International and national players compete with regional banks and new online banks


Revenue Breakdown
  1. Interst
  2. Fixed or Variable Fees (trading commissions, M&A fees, asset management fees)
  3. Premiums

Cost Breakdown
  1. IT (back-end processing, security, apps & websites)
  2. Real estate costs (physical branches)
  3. Losses on investments, such as loan defaults
  4. Labour (customer service, commonly off-shore)
  5. Marketing

Important Considerations
  1. Growth of mobile banking: disruption in the industry from Fintech and online banks.
  2. Regulations within the industry – e.g. Dodd-Frank increased capital requirements for banks.
  3. Changes in customer demographics creating a larger market for retirement products.

Retail

Overview - Industry consists of department stores, wholesale retailers, discount stores, specialty retailers, and online retailers. Amazon and other online retailers caused major disruptions in the industry by lowering the operating costs and passing savings to customers. Technology also reduced bargaining power of retailers as customers can easily compare prices online.

Products include credit cards, mortgages, loans, insurance, and checking/savings accounts

Customers can be segmented by income levels, individual vs small businesses vs large businesses

Highly competitive market: International and national players compete with regional banks and new online banks


Revenue Breakdown
  1. Traffic (foot/online)
  2. Conversion rate (visits vs purchase)
  3. Basket size (driven by consumer spending)
  4. Average price per item
  5. Other revenue - After sales service

Cost Breakdown
  1. Cost of goods sold
  2. Returns
  3. Inventory management (storage and stock)
  4. Distribution
  5. Delivery
  6. Labour
  7. Real estate
  8. Online retail - Technology cost

Important Considerations
  1. Seasonality is a big factor in retail sales – large portion of sales occur in holiday season / end of year.
  2. Social media presence has a large impact on brand strength and perceptions.
  3. Omnichannel retail is growing through e-commerce development or acquisition.
Consumer Packaged Goods (CPG)

Overview - Industry consists of household durable and non-durable products. Mature industry and concentrated market in the US with most growth coming from emerging markets. Sales are impacted by type of product – luxury vs necessity. Customers can be retailers or end users.

Products include credit cards, mortgages, loans, insurance, and checking/savings accounts

Customers can be segmented by income levels, individual vs small businesses vs large businesses

Highly competitive market: International and national players compete with regional banks and new online banks

Revenue Breakdown
  1. Sales direct to consumers (higher margins)
  2. Sales to retailers (lower margins)

Revenue Drivers
  1. Shelf placement in stores vs competition (especially for private lables etc..)
  2. Packaging and price tiering
  3. Product portfolio (cannibalization vs complementary products)

Cost Breakdown
  1. Cost of goods sold (raw materials)
  2. Manufacturing facilities
  3. Packaging
  4. Distribution & inventory management
  5. Marketing
  6. R&D - new product innovation
  7. Environmental and regulatory management
  8. Durables - spoilage

Important Considerations
  1. Discounts and price promotions have lowered margins.
  2. Cannibalisation can be a concern when introducing new products.
  3. Tariffs and regulations play a role in imports/exports and where the firm is manufacturing or sourcing.
